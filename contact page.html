About Me

Welcome to my wonderful website. If you are new here, please take a look around. You might just find something interesting about me.

I work in coastal environmental restoriation at Pontchatrain Conservancy. There, I am the Coastal Data Sharing Program Developer and Technology Coordinator. Part of my job requires handling data requests as they come in and packaging different data sets (either the raw salinity data or interpolated salinity contours) for the end user. Folks who use the data are primarily other non profits, government agencies, and academic institutions. Sometimes we get requests from commercial and recreational fisher-folk who are trying to get a sense of the salinity and where the fish might be biting.

Another aspect of my job is to assist different departments with their technology needs. This might mean updating a webpage (using wordpress, and maybe soon actual HTML/CSS and javascript in the near future!)vor the gift shop, creating maps for tree identification, or operating a drone for restoration monitoring.

Recently, I've been helping the education department with a marine debris project, specifically the data collection, interpretation, and visualization. You can learn more about this project here

Projects

Before learing HTML/CSS and Javascript, I had been teaching myself Python. It is a useful language for data analysis and for writing scripts to automate tedious tasks. Recently, I wrote a script that bulk imports salinty shapefiles into ArcGis Pro and uploads it to an online server, with the idea that people that need that data can go online and fetch it themselves, without having a dedicated person to handle the data requests. That project is here if you want to see how I did it.

I'm also currently the only FAA licensed drone pilot at the organization. Besides looking very interesting, drone footage is useful for community and public outreach. Below is an example of a planting site around the Manch ac landbridge near Ruddock, LA.
